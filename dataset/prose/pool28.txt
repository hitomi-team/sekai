You are the commander of the largest warship in the world, German battleship Bismarck. It is May of 1941. You are going to command your ship to victory over the enemy fleets. You stand in the command room looking out at the open ocean and see a massive battle taking place between two opposing fleets. The British Empire has been fighting for nearly four years against the Axis powers who have been invading their shores with increasing ferocity.

You command everyone to battle stations and prepare for battle against the British Empire.

The British Empire has been trying to defend its territory from the Axis forces that have invaded it since before you were even born. You know this because you are one of them!

You command the ship to aim guns starboard side and fire at any British ship.

Your crew members work feverishly to aim their guns at any potential enemy. They frantically work to reload and fire upon the enemy.

The British battleship HMS Hood opens fire on the Bismarck. Shells landing in the water and some going over the battleship you are on, Splashing water on deck. This might be a problem.

You order the guns to be fixed, aiming them forward and opening fire on the enemy. After about an hour of direct combat, You see the HMS Hood explode.

You stare in awe at the massive explosion.

You command the crew to focus fire on the British battleship HMS Prince of Wales.

Fires and explosions light up the sky as shells land on and around the HMS Prince of Wales. You see a huge gash in her armor, through which you can see into her vital systems.

You say "Fire on that position, The battleship is weak!"

Your crew obeys without hesitation, aiming their guns at the perfect angle to unleash more punishment on the fallen ship.

The HMS Prince of Wales explodes and sinks to the bottom of the ocean.

You grab the helm and continue southeast.

You continue sailing with your newly acquired command. For the rest of the day, you sail southeast until nightfall. You order double watches to be scheduled and to be prepared to fight at a moments notice.

You check the radars.

Two powerful sets of radar sweeps ship and aircraft. They are continuing to approach. You order the engines to be turned on full ahead in response.

The ships speeds up and turns east.

You grab binoculars and search for enemies.

You focus in on the approaching horde of fully prepared ships. Two Royal Navy carriers, two light fleet carriers, six battleships and a dozen cruisers painting the ocean red.

They are continuing their steady approach.

You command the crew to open fire on the Royal navy carrier.

The first salvo erupts from the cannons, tearing through the hull of the carrier. 

You command the Anti-Air gunners to fire at incoming torpedo bomber planes.

Three torpedo planes headed straight towards the ship are shot down by your gunners.

You notice the horde of ships have stopped, they are awaiting your next move.

You commands the crew to fire a salvo into the lead ship.

Your massive volley of shells and bombs lands right into the heart of the lead carrier. There is a huge explosion that tears through the carrier, which quickly goes down.

You command the ship to go full speed ahead and fire at will.

You turn to see a single cruiser coming at your ship head on. It opens fire with everything it has, and your return fire rip through its thick armor, but do nothing to it.

You command all 4 turrets to aim and fire a salvo into the cruiser.

Your massive volley lands right into the side of the cruiser, which topples over and begins to sink. It takes about 3 minutes for it to reach the bottom of the ocean.

You say "Did we take any hits?"

You were too engrossed in firing and didn't notice any damage. It appears that you have made it out of this encounter without a scratch.

You command the ship to turn back and attempt to evade the enemy.

You notice a large gap has opened up in between the enemy group. You order full speed ahead and maneuver your way through the gap. The enemy follows, still firing at your ship.

You command to fire at the enemy battleship King George V.

A huge hole opens up in the side of the battleship, and even though it doesn't sink, it continues moving towards the rear and turns around to face you.

You hail cruiser Prinz Eugen and commands them to fire at battleship King George V.

The cruiser responds with a heavy barrage of artillery and gunfire. The massive caliber bullets ripping through the armor of the battleship and doing major damage to it, making it hard to keep it pointed at you.

You fire a final salvo into the HMS King George V.

The massive explosion causes the HMS King George V to explode spectacularly, tearing a hole in the side of the cruiser, but the Prinz Eugen pulls away from the battle unscathed.

There was no more enemy ships left. It should be smooth sailing from here.

You are congratulated by your staff for an excellent battle.

You command the ship to continue west.

The next day you approach the coast of Africa. The coast stretching on either side is littered with remnants of German ships and boats that tried to land on the continent but were destroyed by the Royal Air Force.

You say "What happened here!"

Your Staff Commander looks at the carnage and says "The Luftwaffe did their best to support the ground troops by air but the RAF was relentless. You see the results of their work."

You say "Tell all the Anti-Air gunners to man positions and get ready for a fight"

You wait expectantly for an attack but none comes. You send a clear message to any potential landings that they would meet the same fate if they tried. You know the crews man the guns are ready and waiting.

You search for any enemy ships.

The coast is clear, there are no ships to be found.

You order the ship to anchor off the coast of Africa and wait for supplies.

The ship navigates to an isolated dock in the port of Lome, a small coastal town on the shores of the Atlantic Ocean.

You walk into the captain's cabin and sit down on the bed.

You see maps and charts covering every wall space and a large globe on the globe itself. You sit and think about your next move.

You get on the radio and attempt contact with Berlin.

You contact the nearest Luftwaffe base in the hopes of getting some new supply information but there is no response. Indeed there is no response from any German station, you receive nothing but static.

You stand up and walk back to the command room.

You sit back and think about your next move. There are choices to be made, none of them easy.

You walk to all of the senior command officers.

You brief them all on the situation and ask for suggestions. You are all stunned into silence. After a few moments one of the officers stands up and suggests that you continue west.

You give orders to continue west.

The ship navigates away from the coast and out to the open sea.

You have managed to get your forces further west than they have ever been and now face the problem of getting supplies to your men.

You command a scout plane to search for enemy supply ships.

The plane spots a convoy of supply ships but they are too far away to do anything about them. The scout pilot is killed in the attack.

You command the ship to follow the supply ships.

The ship navigates closer to the convoy but the distance is too great to make a safe approach.

The radio operator manages to contact the convoy and give a fake order to change course.

You wait silently for the convoy to be in range.

The enemy ships turn away from the ship and begin a slow turn towards it.

The ship navigates toward the turn but you have been moving too slow and behind the ships.

You say "Are we in range?"

The gunners report that they are in range but that they would need at least 1 more minute.

You watch the vessels get closer and closer and prepare to give a command.

You give the command to fire as soon as possible.

The first ship shells the side of your ship. You return fire with your minimal armament and a small amount of explosive fuel that was stored in the shell rooms.

You command a salvo onto the enemy ship with the main guns.

The first shell hits the ships bridge and you see a raging inferno emerge as the ship quickly begins to sink.

You command to approach the enemy convoy at full speed.

The second ship begins moving at flank speed while the first ship begins firing again. A salvo hits the ships starboard side creating a huge gash in the side of the vessel.

You fire the secondary guns into the convoy ships.

You fire a spread of explosive bullets that hit six of the seven ships. The last ship manages to avoid direct hits and break away.

You command each convoy ship to be searched for supplies.

The first ship is searched and 1,000 rounds of 7.62mm ammunition is found which had already been used in the gun.

You command the ship to leave.

The ships return to the intended course and motors are started.

You begin planning a route for the next mission.

1 hour later...

You brief the crew on the next plan of attack.

The ship navigates close to the coast and begins searching houses and buildings in a small fishing port.

You give the order to steal any food from the fishing ports.

Men begin searching and quickly find amazing amounts of food.

The first mission was a success.

The next mission involves doing a little sabotage on the enemy radar site.

The operator informs you that their radios are down and that you should return to your vessel as your squadron is the only thing keeping the enemy away from the main islands.

You arrive at the command room where several operators are performing maintenance and adjustments to the ships equipment.

A sailor is repairing a pipe that had developed a leak earlier.

You say "Hows the pipe working?"

The sailor looks up at the Captain and quietly reports "It's working Captain, but we're not able to make the leak go away just yet'

You say "Good. I'm proud of you"

The sailor looks up at you and half smiles and half frowns.

You notice blood on his hands, he looks away and drips more blood on to the floor.

You say "What happened to you?"

The sailor looks at his hands and begins to explain "I was working on the radar and had to cut open an electrical cable. It took a little longer than expected so I didn't finish the repair on the pipe."

You say "Go to the medic's office and get yourself healed. That's an order"

The sailor nods and leaves.

The crew begins gathering at their stations as the squadron leaders call the roll to finalize their orders.

You approach one of the senior officers who is checking the row of men.

The officer turns and stares at you.

The officer gives a curt nod and strides off the upper deck.

You follow him and hear him yelling instructions to the radiomen. You also hear him telling the men to prepare for anti-ship tactics.

You walk to the boiler rooms.

You follow the officer into a room filled with men and machines. Entirely automated systems take the place of men for operating the more complicated mechanisms.

You say "Everyone. I have a important announcement"

The men begin to listen as you speak.

"We tried to make contact sir, but all we received was a lot of static."

"We think they're holding her in a coastal defense system near your current position."
